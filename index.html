Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a car wrap cost in San Diego?

A full color change wrap typically ranges from $2,500 to $6,000+ depending on the vehicle size, wrap material, and complexity. Luxury and exotic vehicles may cost more due to intricate body lines. Contact us for a free estimate tailored to your vehicle.

How long does a vinyl wrap last?

A high-quality vinyl wrap lasts 5–7 years with proper care. We use premium films from 3M, Avery Dennison, and Hexis that are backed by manufacturer warranties. Hand washing and avoiding prolonged sun exposure will maximize your wrap's lifespan.

Will a car wrap damage my paint?

No — a professionally installed vinyl wrap actually protects your factory paint. When removed, the paint underneath is preserved in its original condition, which can help maintain or even increase your vehicle's resale value.

How long does it take to wrap a car?

Most full wraps take 3–5 business days. This includes surface preparation, bumper and trim removal, precision installation, and reassembly. We never rush — quality takes time.

What's the difference between PPF and a vinyl wrap?

Paint protection film (PPF) is a clear, self-healing film designed to protect your paint from rock chips and scratches. A vinyl wrap changes your vehicle's color or finish. Many customers combine both — PPF on high-impact areas and a wrap for the color change.

Do you wrap Teslas?

Yes — we specialize in Tesla wraps. Tesla's single-stage paint is thinner and softer than most manufacturers, so proper prep is critical. We've wrapped hundreds of Model 3s, Model Ys, Model S, and Model X vehicles.
